TV denoising (test_tvdn)

This example is about total-variation denoising. Given a noisy image $u$, denoising is formulated as a convex optimization problem:

$$ \min_{x} \|x-u\|_2^2 + \lambda TV(x) $$

where $x$ is the "clean"/denoised image. The first term ensures that the resulting image is "close" to the original $u$. The second term -- total variation -- is weighted by a penalty $\lambda$ and is used to penalize pixelwise image differences. It can be written as (anisotropic version): $$ TV(x) = \sum_{i,j} |x_{i+1,j}-x_{i,j}|+|x_{i,j+1}-x_{i,j}| $$ or as (isotropic version): $$ TV(x) = \sum_{i,j} \sqrt{ |x_{i+1,j}-x_{i,j}|^2+|x_{i,j+1}-x_{i,j}|^2 } $$ This optimization problem is convex. Here it is solved using first-order primal-dual algorithm (see references). The problem is fairly parallelizable and some parallelization is done using SSE instructions.

Examples

A sample call:

./test_tvdn -input=../images/valley.jpg -lambda=5 -max_iters=500 -dx_tolerance=1e-3
